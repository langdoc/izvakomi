  • 06/14/15 Fieldwork in Nenetsia

    Some month ago we returned from two weeks long fieldwork period in Nenetsia. The settlements we worked in were Naryan-Mar, Hongurey and Krasnoye. We are currently preparing the data collected, we will soon be able to send ready videos to the informants. Transcribing is also ongoing work, naturally.

  • 06/13/15 Fieldwork in Ižma

    We have planned three different fieldwork trips for the time between 2014 and 2016. The first trip was carried out in March 2014 in Ižma region of Komi Republic. The original plan was to work only outside the Komi Republic, but in the end it made more sense to start our work in more familiar lands. Two members of our team, Marina Fedina and Vasiley Chuprov, are both from Ižma region which gave us a huge advantage in planning the work. It was also good to start easily as we also had to get to know each others. This was anyway just the first time our team was working together.

  • 06/12/15 Welcome to the site!

    This is the website of a language documentation project funded by the Kone Foundation (Finland) which documents the Izhva dialect of the Komi language. This site contains material about our work and how it progresses. As the project lasts three years, it is among the largest documentation projects done with the Uralic languages. We focus heavily on metadata and archiving, making sure that the end users, be they linguists or native Komi, will have easy and intuitive access to our data.

  • Project leaders: Rogier Blokland, Michael Rießler and Marina Fedina
